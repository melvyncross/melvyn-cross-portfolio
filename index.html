Chapter I The Beginning

November 2020.

No storefront. No catalogue. No suppliers. A company that wanted to sell solar online and didn't yet know how.

I joined Planet Soar as their e-commerce lead. Within months I had a temporary contract turn into an apprenticeship, and an apprenticeship turn into the build of planetsoarshop.com. What started as a side channel became the company's commercial engine.

Six years later, the online channel accounts for 60% of revenue and sources 100% of inbound demand. It is a story about scale, but also about protecting what works while everything around it changes.

Chapter II

What I built.

A Shopify storefront with 544 active SKUs at peak, served by nine European suppliers, supported by a custom shipping engine, and run by a team of seven.

0 SKUs at catalogue peak

Panels, batteries, inverters, EV chargers, accessories. Five categories, each with their own logistics constraints.

0 European suppliers onboarded

ENECSOL, VOLTANEO, KRANNICH, BAYWARE, ESTG, POWR CONNECT, ALLIANTZ, plus two irregular partners in Switzerland and Italy.

0 Shipping profiles, hand-built

The problem nobody wanted to solve: per-item shipping across nine suppliers with no carrier API. I built an internal cost model with ~90% accuracy.

0 Daily organic impressions at peak

~500 daily clicks, 2.5% CTR. All organic SEO. Paid was reserved for product launches and peak seasons only.

0 People on the team

Five sales representatives, one e-commerce assistant, plus oversight of the accounting function. KPIs, reporting cadence, process design.

0 Revenue through the online channel

Quotation funnel above €7,000. Direct conversion below. A deliberate split for a high-AOV catalogue, not a workaround.

Chapter IV Oct 17 — Nov 1, 2025

A two-week mission to Southern Africa.

Planet Soar was bidding on a European Union FASEP public-sector contract in Southern Africa. The bid needed local partners on the ground.

I flew to Gaborone and Johannesburg. Two weeks of meetings, site visits, partnership conversations. By the end, we had secured Solar Power Kgalagadi Resources Development Co. as the local partner underpinning the bid.

It is the part of the job that is rarely on the e-commerce manager's brief. I think that's why it matters.
